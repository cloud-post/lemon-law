Understanding California Lemon Law


What is California Lemon Law?

The Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act, commonly referred to as the California Lemon Law, serves as a comprehensive safeguard for consumers who acquire or lease faulty vehicles. This legislation mandates that manufacturers must either repurchase or replace vehicles afflicted with substantial defects that cannot be rectified after a reasonable number of repair attempts.



Who Does California Lemon Law Apply To?

The scope of the California Lemon Law encompasses all individuals who acquire a new or pre-owned vehicle, whether through purchase or lease, within the geographical boundaries of the state. This broad coverage extends to various types of automobiles such as cars, trucks, SUVs, and select recreational vehicles.


The Lemon Law Process


1. Identifying Vehicle Defects

It is imperative to record and communicate any faults found in your vehicle to the manufacturer or dealership as the initial course of action. Maintaining meticulous documentation of all repair endeavors is highly recommended.


2. Qualifying for Lemon Law Protection

In order to be eligible for Lemon Law protection, it is necessary for your vehicle to have undergone a reasonable number of repair attempts and for the issues to significantly hinder its use, value, or safety.


3. Consultation with a Lemon Law Attorney

It is crucial to obtain legal advice. A skilled lawyer will evaluate your situation and provide expert guidance on the most suitable steps to take.


4. Negotiation with the Manufacturer

Your legal representative will engage in negotiations with the manufacturer to reach a settlement, which could potentially include options such as a vehicle buyback or replacement.



5. Filing a Lawsuit

In the event that negotiations prove unsuccessful, your attorney has the option to initiate legal proceedings in order to uphold your rights as stipulated by the Lemon Law.
