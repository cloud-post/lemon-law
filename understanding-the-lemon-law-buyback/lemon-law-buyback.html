What Is A Lemon Law Buyback?



A Lemon Law buyback occurs when a manufacturer repurchases a defective vehicle from the consumer. This buyback is typically the result of multiple failed attempts to repair the vehicle under warranty. Once the manufacturer repurchases the vehicle, it is considered a "lemon law buyback" and is required by law to have a specific title designation.


How Does California Lemon Law Protect Consumers?

The California Lemon Law provides protection to consumers who purchase or lease new or used vehicles that turn out to be lemons. The law requires manufacturers to repurchase or replace a defective vehicle if certain criteria are met. These criteria include:

  • The vehicle has a substantial defect that is covered by the warranty
  • The defect cannot be fixed after a reasonable number of repair attempts
  • The defect substantially impairs the vehicle's safety, value, or use

If these criteria are met, the consumer is entitled to a refund or replacement vehicle. The amount of the refund is typically the purchase price minus any deductions for use. Additionally, the manufacturer may be required to pay the consumer's attorney fees and costs.


What Types Of Vehicles Are Covered Under California Lemon Law?

California Lemon Law covers new and used vehicles that are purchased or leased for personal, family, or household use. The law covers cars, trucks, SUVs, motorcycles, and some RVs. However, the law only applies to vehicles that are still covered by the manufacturer's warranty. If the vehicle is out of warranty, the Lemon Law does not apply.
