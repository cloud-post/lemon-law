What You Need To Know About Lemon Law Buybacks


If you are in the market for a new car, you should familiarize yourself with lemon law buybacks. Many new car buyers may not be aware of lemon law buybacks and the implications they can have on the purchase of a vehicle. Lemon law buybacks are vehicles previously owned by someone who has exercised their rights under the state’s lemon law and have been returned to the manufacturer due to a major defect.

It is important for potential car buyers to be aware of these lemon law buybacks as they may not have been adequately fixed before being resold by the manufacturer. By understanding the lemon law buyback process, car buyers can make an informed decision when considering purchasing a vehicle that may have been previously affected by a lemon law claim. Lemon Law Buyback Vehicle:



A Lemon Law buyback vehicle is a motor vehicle that has been resold by the manufacturer, on or after January 1, 1996, due to a deformity or a wear defect covered under the manufacturer's original warranty. The vehicle must be registered in the manufacturer's name prior to resale to a member of the public.

Requirements manufacturer requires:

The manufacturer must have the following:

  • Request that the California Certificate of Title and registration certificate be marked California Lemon Law Buyback.
  • List the manufacturer of the vehicle in the manufacturer's name.

Attach a removable decal to the vehicle, indicating "Lemon Law Buyback." The decal can be attached in one of the following locations.

  • The exact left door frame.
  • The frame of the main doorway of an automobile, such as the front right-hand door of a motor home.
  • Motorcycle on the left side that does not have a door.

Requirements Seller requires:

When a vehicle covered by Lemon Law is bought back, the seller must notify the buyer on paper in letter-size form, including the following information:

  • Based on the year, make, model, and vehicle identification number (VIN).

  • The title of the car is Lemon Law Buyback.

  • The individual's assessment of the vehicle's original flaw as reported by the purchaser or lessee of the vehicle.

  • Repairs made to an automobile to fix each of its flaws.

Can I tell if the vehicle was a lemon law buyback?

  • Check the Decal section in the Manufacturer Requirements page.
  • California Certificate of Title and registration certificate can help you see to it that the wording of the vehicle is contained in the California Lemon Law Buyback.
  • Do Inquire to know whether this auto was Lemon Law buyback. Law forces the car dealer to inform you when it was.


If I purchase a lemon law buyback vehicle, what is required to obtain it?

Before purchasing a Lemon Law buyback vehicle, the seller informs you in writing of any potential defects indicated by the previous owner.

If you do choose to purchase the vehicle, you should bring transfer documents and warranties. This will include the:

  • California State of Title Certificate.

  • Applicable registration, transfer, and use fees.

  • Smog certification, if appropriate.

  • In the United States, a vehicle odometer disclosure is required for vehicles less than 10 years old. The Vehicle Vessel Transfer and Reassignment Form (REG 262) is the required document.

Overall, lemon law buybacks can be a great way for consumers to get a refund or a replacement vehicle after experiencing repeated issues with a car. They offer a legal and cost-effective way to get back at least some of the money paid for a car that has become a lemon. Consumers should always familiarize themselves with their state's lemon law and any buyback programs available to them.
