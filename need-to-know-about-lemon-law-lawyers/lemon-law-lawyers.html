What Is The Lemon Law In California?




The Lemon Law in California is a state law that provides legal protection to consumers who have purchased a defective vehicle. The law applies to new and used vehicles, including cars, trucks, motorcycles, and recreational vehicles. According to the law, if a vehicle has a substantial defect that cannot be repaired after a reasonable number of attempts, the manufacturer must either replace the vehicle or refund the purchase price.

The California Lemon Law has some of the strongest consumer protection provisions in the United States. The law provides for significant damages to consumers, including refunds of the purchase price, reimbursements for incidental damages, and attorney fees. California is one of the few states that also provides consumers with the option of pursuing a claim in arbitration instead of filing a lawsuit.
