In California, What Are The Criteria For A Car To Be Considered A Lemon?


In California, the Lemon Law empowers consumers to file a claim if a new or pre-owned motor vehicle in the state fails to meet the manufacturer's warranty.

To be eligible for the state lemon law, it is necessary to prove that a reasonable number of repair attempts were made on the car. Usually, four trips to the repair shop are enough to establish that your vehicle is a lemon. A defective car should not require more than two repairs. If your car has been in the shop for more than thirty days, you may have a valid claim under the lemon law.
