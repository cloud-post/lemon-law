Types Of Vehicles And Defects Covered By The Lemon Law


  • The California lemon law, also known as the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act, covers new and used vehicles, including cars, trucks, vans, and SUVs, that are sold or leased in the state of California. The law also applies to motorcycles, recreational vehicles, and motorhomes.
  • The law covers defects that substantially impair the use, value, or safety of the vehicle. These defects must be reported to the manufacturer or its authorized dealer during the vehicle's warranty period and should not have been repaired after a reasonable number of attempts.
  • The law also covers defects that occur within the first 18 months or 18,000 miles (whichever comes first) of ownership, regardless of whether the vehicle is still under warranty.
