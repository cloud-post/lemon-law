What Is the California Lemon Law?

The California lemon law is a statute that provides remedies to customers' who experience problems within their vehicle that they have bought. Each state's laws are slightly different, providing varying options like repurchasing the car, or compensation if it so happens that the issue has deducted value from your vehicle.

What Qualifies as a Lemon?

A lemon is defined as a vehicle with a continuous impairment to the use, value and safety. A vehicle must:

  • Have a defect that is covered by the manufacturer's warranty.
  • Have the defect occur within a specified period of time or a specified number of miles as prescribed by your state.
  • Still not be fixed after reasonable, repetitive repair efforts.

Are Used Cars Covered Under Lemon Law?

Most state lemon laws cover new cars. However, if your used car is still covered under the manufacturer's warranty and you have been back to the shop for repetitive repairs, you may still have rights under the Federal 'Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act'. 

Representation under this act is free of charge.
