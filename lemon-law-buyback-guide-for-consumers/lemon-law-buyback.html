Understanding California Lemon Law Buyback:
A Guide For Consumers

If you have recently purchased a vehicle in California and find that it has persistent defects that cannot be repaired, you may be entitled to a refund or replacement under the California Lemon Law Buyback. The Lemon Law protects consumers who have purchased a vehicle that has a substantial defect that cannot be repaired within a reasonable number of attempts. Pursuing a buyback can be a complex process, but it is important to understand your rights and options.

What Is California Lemon Law Buyback?



California Lemon Law Buyback is a legal remedy for consumers who have purchased a defective vehicle. The California Lemon Law requires manufacturers to repurchase or replace a vehicle that meets certain criteria. The criteria include a substantial defect or nonconformity that the manufacturer or authorized dealership cannot repair within a reasonable number of attempts.


How Does California Lemon Law Buyback Work?

If a consumer believes their vehicle qualifies for a Lemon Law Buyback, they must first provide the manufacturer with written notice of the problem and an opportunity to repair it. If the manufacturer or authorized dealership cannot repair the problem within a reasonable number of attempts, the consumer may be entitled to a repurchase or replacement of the vehicle.

A repurchase occurs when the manufacturer buys back the defective vehicle from the consumer for the full purchase price, including taxes, license fees, and any finance charges paid by the consumer. The manufacturer is also required to reimburse the consumer for any reasonable expenses incurred as a result of the defect, such as towing or rental car costs.

A replacement occurs when the manufacturer provides the consumer with a comparable new vehicle, or a vehicle of the same model and year with comparable features and options. The manufacturer is responsible for all costs associated with the replacement, including taxes, license fees, and any finance charges.


How To Pursue A California Lemon Law Buyback

If you believe that your vehicle meets the criteria for a Lemon Law buyback in California, there are several steps you should take to pursue your claim:


1. Keep Detailed Records

Make sure to keep detailed records of all repairs and maintenance performed on your vehicle, as well as any problems or issues you've experienced. This information will be crucial in proving that your vehicle meets the Lemon Law criteria.


2. Contact the Manufacturer

Contact the manufacturer or their authorized dealer to report the defects and request repairs. Make sure to do this in writing, and keep a copy of all correspondence.



3. Allow for Reasonable Repair Attempts

Under California law, the manufacturer must be given a reasonable opportunity to repair the defects before a Lemon Law buyback can be pursued. This typically means allowing the manufacturer to attempt repairs at least two or three times.


4. Hire an Attorney

If the manufacturer refuses to repurchase the vehicle or offer a reasonable settlement, you may need to hire an attorney who specializes in Lemon Law cases. An attorney can help you navigate the legal process and ensure that your rights are protected.
