What Is The California Lemon Law?


The California Lemon Law is a set of statutes that provide legal remedies for consumers who have purchased defective vehicles. Under the law, a vehicle is considered a "lemon" if it meets certain criteria, such as having a defect that substantially impairs its use, value, or safety, and the defect cannot be repaired after a reasonable number of attempts.

If you believe you have purchased a lemon, the first step is to contact a Lemon Law attorney. Our lawyers will evaluate your case and help you understand your rights under the law. We will work with you to gather the necessary documentation and evidence to support your claim, including repair records, purchase documents, and any correspondence with the dealership or manufacturer.

The Lemon Law applies to new and used vehicles, including cars, trucks, and motorcycles, as well as boats and recreational vehicles. If you believe you have purchased a lemon, it is important to contact a Lemon Law lawyer to understand your legal options and protect your rights as a consumer.
