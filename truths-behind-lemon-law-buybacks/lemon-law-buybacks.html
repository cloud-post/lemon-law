What Defines A Lemon Car?


In order to be considered a lemon, a car must have a significant defect that affects the vehicle's operation, safety, or value, as indicated by a very specific time or distance. The flaw must be covered by a guarantee, and remain unfixed after a reasonable number of attempts. The number of attempts believed to be reasonable varies by state, but usually it is dependent on the issue.

Generally, lemons must be fixed to meet these repair qualifications:
