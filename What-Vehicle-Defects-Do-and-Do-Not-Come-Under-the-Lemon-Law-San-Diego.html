In California, the lemon law is in effect to protect vehicle buyers and leasers who unknowingly bought a defective car. Defects often show up after driving for just one day or about 15 miles. The California Lemon Law helps repair these defects at no cost to you if they are deemed as major by an arbitrator appointed under this act. How Do You Define a Defect in California Lemon Law?

Generally, you can prove if your vehicle is a lemon or not by showing that there are material defects in the car. Material defects affect how safe and practical an automobile is to drive - so be sure to check for these before driving off the lot!

Defect of Use:  The vehicle is not designed to be driven the way you are driving it.

Defect of Value: "You can't sell the vehicle for what it's worth if there are no flaws."

Defect of Safety: You cannot use the vehicle on roads if it affects your and others safety.

There are many defects that are covered under the lemon law, however, these are some exceptions:

Negligence of the Owner of the Vehicle

The lemon law is designed to protect you from unknowingly making a purchase of an expensive vehicle with hidden defects. You can avoid this costly mistake and the accompanying headache by taking the time to do your research before buying any car, new or used!

Car Accidents

If your vehicle is under the lemon law, but it's been damaged by an accident before you've bought or leased it, then that won't be protected.

Poor Repairs and Installations

What Defects Come Under the Lemon Law San Diego California?

The California lemon law requires a manufacturer to replace the vehicle or repurchase the vehicle if they are not able to repair it on time under their original warranty after a reasonable number of attempts.

Proper documentation of your repairs will help you file a lemon law case against the manufacturer and is vital to any attempts at getting justice in court.

These are some vehicle defects that come under the California Lemon Law:

  • Engine Fires
  • Engine Failures
  • Fuel Leakages
  • Steering Issues
  • Misaligned Tires
  • Steering Pull
  • Stalling
  • Brake System Defects
  • Airbag Defects
  • Pedal Defects
  • Body Defects
