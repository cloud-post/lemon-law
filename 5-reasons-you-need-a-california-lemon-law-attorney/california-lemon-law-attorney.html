5 Reasons You Need a California Lemon Law Attorney and
What They Can Do For You

If a new car turns out to be really, really lemony, then you’ll be eligible for a full or partial refund. The laws in California are, very loosely, called the “Lemon Law”, or “Lemon Aid Law”, and it’s important to know your rights if you suspect you have purchased or leased a car that turns out to be defective. California Lemon Law attorneys are worth their weight in gold—especially if you are dealing with one of the car brands listed below.

    1. No fees

If you think your car is a lemon, the consultation with a lemon law lawyer is free. They will let you know if filing a claim to seek compensation from the manufacturer or retailer of your vehicle makes sense for your particular circumstance and thereby whether it’s worth proceeding. What’s more, if one does succeed in their claim and is deemed eligible for compensation, California Lemon Laws stipulate that consumers receive a refund of their money and the auto manufacturer/retailer pays all attorneys fees and costs, not them.

    3. Cutting through red tape

Most people do not understand what they need to do in order to file an insurance claim or even how the claims process works. This can be frustrating and stressful and lead one to getting very confused about what steps to take next. A good insurance claims attorney helps guide the applicant through the process of filing their claim so that all deadlines are met and any possible legal repercussions from pursuing a claim are minimized.

    4. Getting the right offer

One way car dealerships make people believe they're getting a ‘great deal’ is by offering a low amount of money on the vehicle they want to trade in. They know that most car owners feel like the price proposed is fair and take it before ever finding out what the real value of their vehicle is worth. Make sure you avoid this lure of low-balling and have your attorney look into all options related to your used car's trade-in value because you'll likely find it differs from what was originally proposed by the salesman at the dealership .
