Lemon Law Solutions


If you reside in a state that follows lemon law, you are entitled to these particular key solutions:



Vehicle Replacement

Your dealership will provide another vehicle that is similar in value and options. Your new vehicle will come with the same dealer options as your first. In some instances, the dealer will offer you the same service contract. You may be entitled to some form of reimbursement if the car was towed or you rented another vehicle while yours was being repaired after an accident.

Keep in mind that you may be liable for the price of used parts if you owned the vehicle without an issue for a given period of time. A lemon law attorney will be able to supply you with the skilled representation required for your unique situation.


Buyback or Refund

You could choose to have your vehicle bought back, and this option consists of the following:

  • Manufacturer refunds your down payment on the vehicle
  • Refund of your monthly payments
  • Reimbursement of your vehicle’s financing, taxes, registration fees, and service contracts (provided by the manufacturer)
  • Paying off the remaining loans and/or lease of your defective purchase

What Is A Cash-And-Keep Lemon Law Settlement?


If your Consumer Protection Act complaint for your damaged automobile does not satisfy your state 's requirements, then your retailer might be required to pay you monetary compensation and keep your vehicle in a cash-and-keep settlement. A cash-and-keep investigation means that you could get to keep your automobile, but you'll receive cash for the problems you've faced as a result of this damaged vehicle. It may be to your interest to speak with an attorney about the usefulness and issue of the cash and receipt plan.



What Are Typical Lemon Law Settlements?


Since each state has its own statutes regarding the details of a settlement, there isn't an atypical scenario of a lemon law lawsuit settlement. Lemon law instances usually take about 30 days to resolve, but some disputes can take up to half a year. It's a good idea to consult an attorney to confirm that your manufacturers do not exploit your lack of knowledge.

If you’re looking to build leverage in your case, then follow these simple steps:

  • Keep detailed notes about repairs, conversations you’ve had with your manufacturer, times, dates, and names, and any other useful information that can help you to build a better claim.
  • Inform your manufacturer as soon as possible that you are intending to start the buyback process once you’ve determined your car meets your states’ criterium.
  • Hire a lawyer that can help you navigate lemon law and litigate your arbitration or case.
  • Research all information about the make and model of your vehicle to determine if the deficiencies of your vehicle are systemic.
