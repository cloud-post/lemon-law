You shouldn't have to worry about your car breaking down if you've purchased or leased a new one. If this is you, it's essential to understand your rights under California's lemon law lawyers.

The lemon law exists to protect consumers in California who have bought or leased a new or used car, truck, motorcycle, or other vehicle from a dealership. This law requires manufacturers to provide a refund or replacement to consumers if their vehicle has serious defects that still exist after multiple repair attempts.

Who is Covered Under the Lemon Laws in California?

The lemon laws in California do not protect every person who owns or leases a vehicle. You must meet certain conditions to qualify for lemon law protection in California. For example, you must have purchased or leased a new or used car, truck, motorcycle, or other vehicle from a dealership. 

In addition, the vehicle must be covered by the manufacturer or dealer warranty. Also, the vehicle must have a defect that substantially impairs its value, use, or safety. If the dealer is unable to fix this defect after multiple repair attempts, the vehicle is most likely a lemon.

If you meet all of these conditions, you could have the right to file a lemon law claim. 

Consumers who do not meet these conditions should not give up hope. Even if you are not covered by California’s lemon law, you could still be entitled to compensation for your defective vehicle. 

Are You Entitled to Compensation For Your Lemon?

California law stipulates that consumers who meet specific conditions are legally entitled to either a refund or a replacement vehicle. If the refund option is chosen, the manufacturer must refund the purchase price, sales tax, down payments, monthly payments, and any other fees associated with the purchase or lease of the vehicle. If the replacement vehicle option is chosen, the replacement vehicle must be a new, defect-free vehicle that is substantially similar to the one currently owned.

If your vehicle is not covered under the state’s lemon laws, you could still be entitled to compensation for your vehicle’s issues.
