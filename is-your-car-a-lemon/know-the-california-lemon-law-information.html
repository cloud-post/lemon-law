What Is The Nature Of The Protection Offered By The California Lemon Laws?

If you have leased or bought a vehicle or other consumer goods for your personal, family, or household use, you could be safeguarded by the California Lemon Laws. In California, if the manufacturer fails to fix the defective product or vehicle after multiple repair attempts, they are obligated to either replace the faulty product or issue a refund.


What Is The Purpose Of Lemon Laws In California?

Certain products in California are subject to lemon laws which require the manufacturer to:

  • Make repair services available in California for easy accessibility.

  • Ensure that the repair of your vehicle or any faulty item is completed within 30 days.

  • A limited number of repair attempts are permitted for manufacturers to rectify any defects in your product.

  • After deducting any trouble-free use values, manufacturers are required to provide you with a replacement or refund.

  • It is the responsibility of the manufacturer to cover your attorney costs and fees that are deemed reasonable.

California's lemon laws may provide you with protection if you have purchased or leased a new or used motor vehicle that includes a manufacturer's warranty.

In case your warranted motor vehicle cannot be fixed by the manufacturer or seller despite multiple repair attempts, they are obliged to cover your incidental and consequential expenses.

It should be emphasized that the number of repair attempts considered reasonable depends entirely on the severity of the defect. However, in general, safety defects should necessitate fewer repair attempts than non-safety issues.



As A Consumer In California, What Rights Do I Have?

Purchasing or leasing a defective vehicle or product may leave you uncertain about your rights, to the extent that you might dispose of it and opt for a replacement instead. The purpose of California lemon laws is to safeguard you against faulty products and offer you recompense.

If you are the owner of a motor vehicle in California, the lemon laws offer you various remedies.

  • The manufacturer has the option to buy back your faulty vehicle, deducting any expenses made on it and factoring in its trouble-free usage value.
  • Get a new vehicle to replace the defective one.
  • Propose monetary compensation.
  • Compensate your attorney's costs and fees that are reasonable.

In the end, the California Lemon Law provides legal recourse for car owners who have been sold or leased a defective vehicle.
