What Is The Lemon Law Buyback Program?



The Lemon Law Buyback program is a great way for Californians to get rid of their lemons. To qualify for a buyback, the vehicle must have a defect that substantially impairs its use, value, or safety, and the manufacturer must have been given a reasonable number of attempts to repair the defect.

If the manufacturer is unable to repair the defect after a reasonable number of attempts, they are required to offer the consumer a buyback option. The consumer can either receive a replacement vehicle or a refund of the purchase price, minus a deduction for the use of the vehicle.

How Does The Lemon Law Buyback Program Work?

If you think your car might be a lemon, the first step is to reach out to a Lemon Law attorney in San Diego. They can help you figure out if your car qualifies for a buyback, and walk you through the process of filing a claim.

Once you have established that your vehicle qualifies for a buyback, your attorney will send a demand letter to the manufacturer. The demand letter will outline the defect in detail and provide evidence of the manufacturer's attempts to repair the defect. The letter will also request a buyback of the vehicle under the Lemon Law Buyback program.

If the manufacturer agrees to the buyback, they will offer you a choice between a replacement vehicle or a refund of the purchase price, minus a deduction for the use of the vehicle. If you choose a replacement vehicle, the manufacturer must provide a vehicle that is substantially identical to the one being replaced. If you choose a refund, the manufacturer must provide the full purchase price of the vehicle, minus the use deduction.
