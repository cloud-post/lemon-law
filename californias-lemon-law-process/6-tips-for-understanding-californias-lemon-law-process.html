      2. What does Lemon Law mean?

    Lemon Law regulates the feasibility of a car's condition versus the number of repairs performed by the manufacturer. There's a law that allows manufacturers a certain number of instances to repair a vehicle. If the total number of repair attempts exceeds the limit:

    • The manufacturer either has to buy the damaged vehicle and replace it with a new, matching one, or
    • Return the money.

      3. Different laws for different vehicles

    If the vehicle is for business purposes, the Lemon Law generally may not apply. If the vehicle falls within the specified gross weight, the Lemon Law may not apply.

      4. What happens if you buy a lemon?

    A "lemon law" refers to a legal provision that allows for a replacement despite a vehicle having issues. Note that you are free to make any replacement for this scenario.
