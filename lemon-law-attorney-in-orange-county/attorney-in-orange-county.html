How Can I Prove My California Car Is A Lemon?



To prove that your car is a lemon in California, you need to show that it has a defect that significantly affects its use, value, or safety and that the manufacturer or dealer has been unable to repair it after a reasonable number of attempts. Here are the steps you can take:

  • Gather evidence: Keep detailed records of all repairs and any correspondence with the manufacturer or dealer. This includes receipts, invoices, and letters.

  • Meet the notification requirement: Notify the manufacturer or dealer in writing of the problem and your intent to seek relief under the lemon law. The manufacturer must be given a reasonable opportunity to repair the defect.

  • File a complaint with the California Bureau of Automotive Repair (BAR) Lemon Law Program: This program provides free mediation services to resolve disputes between consumers and manufacturers. You can file a complaint online at the BAR website.

  • Hire an attorney: If your case is not resolved through the BAR program, you may want to consider hiring an attorney who specializes in lemon law cases.

  • Take the manufacturer to court: If the manufacturer is unable or unwilling to resolve the issue through the BAR program or settlement negotiations, you may need to take legal action and file a lawsuit in court.

How Long Does It Take To Settle A Lemon Law Claim In Orange County, California?


In Orange County, California, the process of settling a lemon law claim can be lengthy, depending on the complexity of the case. Generally, claims must first be submitted to the vehicle manufacturer and its authorized service centers, and the manufacturer is given a reasonable amount of time to attempt to repair the vehicle. 

If the manufacturer is unable to repair the vehicle, the claimant may pursue a lemon law claim. The length of time for a lemon law claim to be settled in Orange County, California varies depending on the number of issues in dispute, the complexity of the case, and the time it takes for all parties to come to an agreement. On average, it can take several months to a year to settle a lemon law claim; however, this can vary greatly depending on the circumstances. 



What Can Consumers Gain From Filing A Lemon Law Claim In Orange County?


Consumers who file a successful lemon law claim in Orange County, California, can gain several remedies including:

  • Replacement vehicle: In some cases, the manufacturer may agree to replace the lemon vehicle with a new one that is free from defects.

  • Refund: Consumers may be entitled to a refund of the purchase price or a percentage of the purchase price, depending on how long they owned the vehicle and the mileage.

  • Monetary compensation: Consumers may be entitled to compensation for expenses incurred as a result of the defect, such as towing fees, rental car costs, and repair costs.

  • Attorney's fees: If you win your lemon law claim, the manufacturer may be required to pay your attorney's fees.

By filing a lemon law claim, consumers can hold the manufacturer accountable for selling them a defective vehicle and ensure that they receive fair compensation for the inconvenience and expenses they incurred.
